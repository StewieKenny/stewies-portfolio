Advertising Campaign Performance Analysis: Uncovering Insights from Social Media Marketing Data

Goal

The objective of this project was to analyze advertising campaign data from multiple social media platforms to understand which channels, audiences, and strategies deliver the best results. Using this data, I aimed to calculate key performance metrics such as ROI, revenue, CTR, and conversion rates, and visualize the findings through clear, actionable insights.


Dataset

The dataset was sourced from Kaggle and includes information on advertising campaigns such as:

  • Campaign ID, goal, target audience, duration
  • Channel used (Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, etc.)
  • Conversion rate, acquisition cost, ROI
  • Clicks, impressions, engagement score
  • Customer segment, language, location, and date


What I Wanted to Find Out

  • Which social media channels deliver the highest ROI and revenue?
  • How do click-through rates and conversion rates vary across campaigns?
  • What optimization opportunities exist to improve future campaigns?

Analysis & Visualizations

Instead of building a full dashboard, I focused on creating clear, individual visualizations to highlight key insights.




Campaign Performance:

Twitter achieved the highest conversion rates in three out of four campaigns, Increase Sales, Market Expansion, and Product Launch, with Instagram outperforming it only in the Brand Awareness campaign. Overall performance remained positive, as average conversion rates showed no significant variation across channels, indicating consistent campaign effectiveness.


Campaign Engagement:

Facebook recorded the highest CTR in the Brand Awareness and Market Expansion campaigns, while Twitter and Instagram led in the Increase Sales and Product Launch campaigns, respectively. Pinterest showed a significantly lower CTR compared to the top three platforms, indicating weaker user engagement on that channel


Acquisition Cost Vs Revenue:

While Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter generated positive revenue relative to their acquisition costs, Pinterest revealed negative returns, highlighting its underperformance as an advertising channel and suggesting that future investment on the platform should be reconsidered.


Conclusion:

The analysis of campaign performance across major social media platforms revealed clear differences in effectiveness. Twitter consistently drove strong conversion rates across most campaign objectives, while Facebook excelled in generating high click-through rates for awareness and expansion initiatives. Revenue analysis showed that Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter delivered positive returns on investment, whereas Pinterest underperformed in both engagement and revenue generation.
These insights highlight the importance of prioritizing high-performing platforms for future campaigns while reassessing investment in underperforming channels. Overall, the findings demonstrate how data-driven evaluation of campaign metrics can guide smarter marketing decisions and more efficient allocation of advertising budgets.
